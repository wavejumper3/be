How to take attendance quickly

So, my professor asked the class -
"What do you think is the best way to take attendance?"
I gave a solution I was fairly proud of, but it didn't get used.
My professor does web development, so, guess what?

I'm gonna make this page an awesome explanation of my proposed solution!

Ok, so, it relies on a fair few assumptions.
One, it assumes that the majority of people in class will be present.
Two, it assumes that mouse clicks and name-calling both take constant time.
There's actually two algorithms I'll explain, as well as the one he used.
I'll explain his first.

This guy's algorithm!!!

So, he took the naiive approach:

Starting condition: all students are marked as "not marked", set index to first student.
  1. Call out that student's name.
  2. If they respond, mark them present (one mouse click)
  3. If they do NOT respond after X calls, mark absent (two mouse clicks)
  4. Move, repeat the above.
  5. Once done, click "submit"

Now, this does make decent time in the majority of cases, and is O(n). More exactly, its O(2n + 2X[num absent]).

However, this can be improved!
Here's...

My originally proposed algorithm!!!

I noted that the software allows you to mark everyone as present, and that most people are present.

Starting condition: all students are marked as "present" (one click), set index to first student.
  1. Call out that student's name. (one click)
  2. If they respond, move on.
  3. If they do NOT respond after X calls, mark absent (one mouse click)
  4. Move, repeat the above.
  5. Once done, click "submit"

Now, this does takes less time in the majority of cases, despite also being O(n). More exactly, though, its O(n + X[num absent] + 1).

2n + 2X[num absent] will usually be larger than n + X[num absent] + 1)

(That's a good thing)


My third algorithm technically adds some optimisation, but requires copying over last class's attendance.
Considering most absences are runs, asking "was anyone not here last class?" to correct last week's absences is faster.
This is because verifying an absence takes much longer than someone who is there.
